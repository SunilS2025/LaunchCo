The Launch Challenge

Why Pharmaceutical Launches Still Struggle

Despite rigorous clinical development and robust scientific evidence, pharmaceutical launches frequently miss their potential—not because of weak science, but because of organizational complexity that overwhelms even the most experienced teams.

The Reality: Cross-functional launch teams coordinate 100+ interdependent deliverables across medical affairs, commercial, regulatory, market access, and operations—spanning multiple markets, therapeutic indications, and stakeholder groups. The compounding complexity creates friction at every turn, delays critical decisions, and obscures true readiness until it's too late.
📊

Fragmented Information Systems

Launch materials scattered across SharePoint sites, email chains, and departmental drives. No single source of truth for readiness status, regulatory approvals, or cross-market dependencies. Teams waste 30% of their time simply finding the information they need to do their work.

⚖️

Inconsistent Quality Standards

What constitutes "launch ready" varies wildly across franchises, markets, and therapeutic areas. Medical education materials lack consistency. Payer value propositions aren't benchmarked. HCP engagement strategies reinvent the wheel with each launch, despite similar precedents.

🔥

Reactive Risk Detection

Critical gaps surface days or weeks before pivotal regulatory milestones. Leadership discovers readiness issues when recovery options are limited. Risk identification depends on individual vigilance rather than systematic early-warning mechanisms.

⏱️

Unsustainable Operational Overhead

Brand teams drowning in program management tasks—status updates, alignment meetings, manual report compilation. Limited ability to leverage templates, frameworks, or learnings from previous launches. Every launch feels like starting from scratch.

🔄

Cross-Functional Misalignment

Medical affairs, commercial, and market access operate with different success definitions and competing priorities. Executives receive conflicting messages from functional leaders. Strategic intent gets lost in translation to tactical execution.

📚

Learning Loss

Post-launch reviews capture valuable insights, but they sit in documents that rarely inform future launches. Institutional knowledge walks out the door with people. Launch Centers of Excellence struggle to systematize best practices at scale.

Launch Architecture

Orchestrating Functional Excellence and Strategic Oversight

Launch Copilot brings structure to both the functional workstreams that create launch deliverables and the oversight mechanisms that ensure cross-functional alignment, quality standards, and strategic coherence.

🔧

Functional Workstreams

  • Medical Affairs: Medical education strategy, scientific communications, publications planning, MSL training, KOL engagement, advisory boards, congress strategy
  • Commercial: Brand positioning, marketing campaigns, omnichannel strategy, sales force training and enablement, patient services design, DTC planning
  • Market Access: Payer engagement strategy, HEOR evidence generation, value dossier development, reimbursement positioning, pricing strategy, pull-through programs
  • Regulatory & Compliance: Regulatory submissions, labeling negotiations, promotional material review, adverse event management, post-marketing commitments
  • Operations & Supply: Supply chain readiness, distribution strategy, demand forecasting, patient support program infrastructure, specialty pharmacy relationships
🎯

Oversight & Alignment Workstreams

  • Launch Governance: Steering committee operations, decision-making frameworks, escalation protocols, cross-functional prioritization, resource allocation
  • Cross-Functional Integration: Dependency identification and management, handoff protocols between teams, integration testing of connected deliverables, conflict resolution
  • Quality Assurance: Standards definition and enforcement, deliverable reviews against benchmarks, compliance verification, brand consistency checks
  • Risk & Issue Management: Systematic risk identification, impact assessment, mitigation planning, recovery scenario development, lessons learned capture
  • Performance & Readiness: KPI definition and tracking, readiness scoring, stakeholder reporting, launch rehearsals, go/no-go decision support
